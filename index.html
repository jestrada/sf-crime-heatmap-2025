Bay Area Open Data Visualizations

I'm learning data visualization by exploring publicly available city government datasets — crime reports, traffic crashes, and more from SF and Oakland. Each project is a self-contained interactive chart built with D3.js. All data comes from DataSF and Oakland Open Data.

🚗 SF Traffic Crashes — Top 10 Streets

San Francisco 2005–2025

Calendar-style heatmap showing weekly injury crash counts on San Francisco's most dangerous streets, year by year. Each year displays its own top 10 streets ranked by total crashes, with 💀 skull markers for weeks with fatalities and stacked bar charts showing injury vs. fatal crash totals.

Goal: I wanted to see which streets were the most dangerous to walk on, using a heatmap to spot weekly patterns at a glance. I also wanted to easily compare street-level totals (via the bar charts) and browse historical data year by year. What's missing: there's no easy way to compare the same street across years — you have to scroll and eyeball it. A small-multiples or overlay view would help.

64,409 crashes 135,617 party records 611 fatal crashes 21 years

🔴 SF Crime Heatmap 2025

San Francisco 2025

Weekly crime incident heatmap across 41 San Francisco neighborhoods. Color intensity shows incident volume per week — from larceny and assault to drug offenses. Tenderloin and South of Market consistently lead.

Goal: My first visualization — wanted to get a feel for which neighborhoods have the most crime and whether there are seasonal spikes. The heatmap makes it easy to spot the Tenderloin as a persistent outlier.

89,954 incidents 41 neighborhoods 52 weeks

🟠 Oakland Crime Heatmap 2025

Oakland 2025

Same concept, across the bay. Weekly crime incidents by Oakland police beat, revealing geographic and seasonal patterns in reported crime.
